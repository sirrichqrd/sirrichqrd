About

With years of experience in Shotokan Karate, Yoga, Tai Chi, Qi-gong, I've guided students, organizations, and wellness seekers in building strength, balance, and mindfulness. My work has connected me with schools, hotels, corporate organizations, and wellness centers, delivering programs that blend traditional practices with modern needs.

My journey began with traditional martial arts and has evolved to encompass a comprehensive understanding of how physical practice, mental training, and spiritual development work together to create lasting transformation.

Hello, I’m Richard Abraham Ugbo-Ojoide — a Martial Artist, Holistic Health Practitioner, and Yoga Instructor, passionate about blending tradition, culture, and modern wellness.

For over a decade, I have dedicated myself to the study and practice of Shotokan Karate, holding a 2nd-degree black belt. My martial arts journey extends beyond Karate, touching disciplines like Aikido, Judo, Tai Chi, and Combat Jujutsu, each shaping how I view discipline, resilience, fitness and self-mastery.

I also work closely with Shihandai ’Femi Johnson, popularly known as the Sensei, Founder and CEO of the sister companies Classical Martial Arts (CMA) and CLEAR (Center for Lifestyle Enhancement and Re-engineering). Under his leadership, I serve as a Senior Associate Instructor at CMA and the Lead Instructor at CLEAR, where we bring both martial arts and holistic health into structured learning experiences for diverse audiences.

At the same time, my path in holistic health has grown into a lifelong calling. As a certified instructor of Yoga, Tai Chi, and Qi-gong, I bring ancient practices into modern spaces—helping individuals and organizations find balance, strength, and clarity. I’ve had the privilege of working with respected establishments including Fraser Suites, Peace Media Hotels, and Blossom Health, providing wellness programs that nurture both body and mind.

My Fitness Philosophy

But beyond techniques and titles, what truly defines me is my commitment to wellness as a way of life. To me, fitness isn’t just about how much you can lift or how long you can hold a plank (though that has its place!). I often share the idea that fitness is about:

  • Being able to carry yourself (your body weight) confidently.
  • Having the stamina to run for your life (literally!) without running out of breath — that’s good cardio.
  • Doing your daily chores without muscle restraints, stiff joints, or cracks — that’s mobility and flexibility.
  • Building arm strength and foot balance so that you’re not reaching for a walking stick at 60.

This isn’t just an idea — it’s the foundation of your fitness journey. And yes, I can guide you there. Whether you’re already walking the path, just taking your first steps, or still standing at the edge and thinking about it, I create personalized structures designed around your unique health and fitness needs. Your journey matters, and I make sure it’s built for you.

Currently, I’m building ITAS (Intensive Training and Seminars), a platform that brings together martial arts, holistic health, leadership, and capacity building into transformative experiences. ITAS is my vision for empowering individuals and communities through skill, discipline, and cultural wellness.

Outside the dojo and studio, I’m also a creative. My background and hobbies in writing (especially poetry), graphic design, UI/UX, mobile photography, and content creation allow me to present ideas in fresh and engaging ways, bridging traditional practices with modern storytelling.

Ultimately, my mission is simple: to serve as a Global Cultural Wellness Diplomat, using martial arts, yoga, and holistic healing to bridge cultures, empower communities, and inspire healthier ways of living.
